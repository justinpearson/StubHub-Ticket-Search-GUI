The Book of Mormon San Jose tickets

Hailed by the New York Times and Washington Post, The Book of Mormon is a Tony Award-winning Broadway satire that pokes fun at organized religion. The San Jose Broadway Company performs The Book of Mormon San Jose, having previously showcased such musicals as Sherlock Holmes, The Lion King and Sunset Boulevard. Fans buying The Book of Mormon San Jose tickets can expect a raucous reception to this critically acclaimed musical.

The Book of Mormon San Jose history

Trey Parker, Robert Lopez and Matt Stone spent eight years creating The Book of Mormon. Parker and Stone have won accolades in the past with their hit series South Park, which won four Emmys. Lopez is a Tony Award winner known for the musical comedy Avenue Q. The Book of Mormon first debuted on Broadway in 2011, and went on to set box-office records at the Eugene O'Neill Theatre.

The Book of Mormon is the winner of nine Tony Awards, including Best Direction of a Musical and Best Book of a Musical. The show also bagged a Grammy for Musical Theatre Album. The show's album peaked at No. 3 on the Billboard charts.

The show has embarked on two successful national tours, including a stop in Salt Lake City, Utah, where the satirical show is based. The 2,700-seat San Jose Center for the Performing Arts plays home to The Book of Mormon San Jose.

The Book of Mormon plot summary

The show is a satirical take on the LDS Church and its sacred text, the Book of Mormon. The story revolves around missionaries Elder Price and Elder Cunningham, who are sent to Uganda, which is rife with poverty, AIDS and other problems.

Tony Award winner Nic Rouleau, who has performed in such musicals as Legally Blonde and Toy Story , plays Price. Christopher John O'Neill, who is a Helen Hayes Award winner and also the winner of Best Actor in a Comedy (New York Television Festival) for his role in The Chris and Paul Show, takes on the role of Cunningham. Other notable cast members include Tony award nominee Daniel Breaker, Lewis Cleale and Stephen Ashfield.
